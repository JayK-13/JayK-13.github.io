About

The Fault In Our Stars is a 2012 young adult novel by author John Green and published by Dutton Books. It's his sixth novel and is narrated by 16-year-old cancer teen Hazel Grace. The two main characters are her and another teen cancer patient, Augustus Waters.

The 2014 film "The Fault In Our Stars" was based off of this book, and was rated 7.9/10 on IMDb. The movie was met with favorable reviews, and won one of the 41st People's Choice Awards, 3 MTV Movie Awards, and all 7 Teen Choice Awards 2014 that it was nominated for.

The title of the book comes from this line in Act 1, Scene 2 of Shakespeare's play Julius Caesar, where Cassius says to Brutus: "The fault, dear Brutus, is not in our stars,/But in ourselves, that we are underlings." Another part influenced by Shakespear was the last line of the book, "I do", symbolizes marriage because "Shakespeare's comedies end in marriage and his tragedies end in death, and I was rather fond of the idea that my book could end (symbolically, at least) in both.
